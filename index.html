Scaling Environments for Agents (SEA)

NeurIPS 2025 Workshop

Date: TBD   |   Location: In-person @ NeurIPS 2025

The SEA Workshop explores the role of environments in developing intelligent agents, especially those powered by large language models (LLMs). Environments are not just testing grounds—they are active components in shaping agent learning, behavior, and autonomy.

  • Build a cross-disciplinary research community.
  • Advance the study of scalable, interactive, and dynamic environments.
  • Treat environments as core components of agent development.

Relevance to NeurIPS: SEA aligns with NeurIPS' mission to understand computational learning and reasoning by emphasizing how scalable environments drive general-purpose intelligence.

Topics of Interest Scaling Environments for Agents
1
Environment Design and Simulation Task formalization, compositionality, affordance modeling, procedural generation, and simulator integration.
2
Benchmarks and Evaluation Multi-step interaction metrics, generalization testing, open-ended benchmarks, curriculum scaling, and human-in-the-loop assessments.
3
LLMs in Interactive Environments Policy learning, planning, reward shaping, hybrid training (e.g., RLHF, PPO), and fine-tuning through interaction.
4
Tool-Use and Software Environments Agents as programmers, API orchestration, tool scaffolding, software manipulation, and web navigation.
5
Multi-Agent and Social Environments Scaling agent populations, emergent behaviors, communication, coordination, competition, and role dynamics.
6
Embodiment and Grounding Perception-action loops, physical simulation, spatial reasoning, robotics integration, and simulation-to-physical grounding.
7
Sim2Real and Deployment Domain adaptation, real-world API integration, robustness under scale, safety, and large-scale deployment.
Call for Papers Scaling Environments for Agents

We invite two submission tracks:

  • Short Papers (up to 4 pages): Position papers, preliminary findings.
  • Long Papers (up to 9 pages): Full research contributions.

All accepted papers will be presented in poster sessions. Select papers will be featured in Spotlight Talks and eligible for the Best Paper Award.

Diversity & Inclusion Scaling Environments for Agents
